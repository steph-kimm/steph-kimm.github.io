A Few Extra-Curriculars

Volunteering at St.Lukes

I volunteer at Mount Sinai St.Lukes hospital one to two times a week. I started my application to volunteer the second that I turned 16 and I was ready to do everything I needed there. We assist the patients in ways that we can, and we try to help out their families as well. When you're a volunteer for long enough, you're even asked to train the new volunteers.

Medical Mentorship Program at Weill Cornell

37 students from the NYC high school area take place in this mentorship a year. We are provided with medical student mentors to teach us about the process they went through and are continuing to go through to become doctors. One of the main things we do, my favorite activity there, is look at a real case that happened. We get the paitent's stats and symptoms, and we try to diagnose the paitent.

Brown Scholars Program

20 kids around New York City learn how to code through python while we apply it to real life situations. We also work with museum staff and help them with their research while learning a lot ourselves.
